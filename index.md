               <br>    
               I am a fifth-year PhD student at the <a href="https://uwaterloo.ca/" class="class0">University of Waterloo</a> in the 
               <a href="https://algcomp.uwaterloo.ca/" class="class0">Algorithms & Complexity Group</a> within the <a href="https://cs.uwaterloo.ca/" class="class0">Cheriton School of Computer Science</a>. 
               I am incredibly fortunate to be advised by <a href="https://sepehr.assadi.info/" class="class0">Sepehr Assadi</a>.
               Before this, I spent three wonderful years at <a href="https://www.rutgers.edu/" class="class0">Rutgers University</a> in the 
               <a href="https://theory.cs.rutgers.edu/" class="class0">theory group </a> of the <a href="https://www.cs.rutgers.edu/" class="class0">CS Department</a>. 
               Prior to this, I was a Computer Science major at <a href="https://camden.rutgers.edu/new/" class="class0">Rutgers Camden</a> for one year, where I was mentored 
               by <a href="https://crab.rutgers.edu/~rajivg/" class="class0">Rajiv Gandhi</a>. 
               I completed three years of my bachelor's in Computer Science Engineering at <a href="https://www.mahindrauniversity.edu.in/" class="class0">Mahindra &Eacute;cole Centrale</a> before 
               transferring to Rutgers Camden.
               <br>    
               <br>    

               My research interest is in Theoretical Computer Science and specifically in Streaming Algorithms. 
               I have recently also worked on Sublinear, Dynamic, and Learning-Augmented algorithms. 
               I generally enjoy working on Graph problems in these settings.
